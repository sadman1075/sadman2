Construction waste

The materials and detritus created during the building, renovation, destruction, or deconstruction of infrastructure are referred to as construction waste. One of the major waste sources in many nations is solid waste from the building sector. According to (Amasuomo & Baird, 2016b), building waste in Hong Kong averaged 29,674.013 metric tons per day. A variety of materials, including concrete, wood, bricks, metal, polymers, insulation, electrical wiring, and others, can be found in this debris.
